What We Offer

Extension & Renewal Services

Comprehensive visa extension solutions tailored to your specific immigration needs in the Philippines.

Tourist Visa Extension

Extend your stay beyond the initial 30 days. Extensions can be granted in 1-6 month increments, up to a maximum of 36 months total stay.

ACR I-Card

Alien Certificate of Registration is required for all foreign nationals staying beyond 59 days. Serves as your official identification in the Philippines.

ECC (Emigration Clearance Certificate)

Required before departing the Philippines if you've stayed for 6+ months. Confirms no outstanding immigration violations or obligations.

Overstay Compliance

If you've exceeded your authorized stay, we help resolve overstay issues including penalty assessment, documentation, and compliance clearance.

Be Prepared

Document Requirements

Ensure you have the necessary documents ready for a smooth and efficient application process.

For Visa Extension

  • Valid passport with current visa stamp
  • Completed BI extension application form
  • Passport-size photos
  • Previous extension receipts (if applicable)

For ACR I-Card / ECC

  • Valid passport with entry stamp
  • ACR I-Card (for ECC applications)
  • Proof of visa extension payments
  • Flight itinerary (for ECC)

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about visa extensions and renewals in the Philippines.

We recommend applying at least 1 week before your visa expires. Applying after expiration results in overstay penalties. We can process urgent extensions if needed.
The Alien Certificate of Registration I-Card is mandatory for all foreign nationals staying in the Philippines beyond 59 days. It serves as your official ID and must be carried at all times.
Overstay penalties vary based on the length of overstay. Monthly fines apply plus additional processing fees. Early resolution minimizes costs. Contact us for a specific assessment.
An ECC is required if you've stayed for 6 months or longer since your last arrival. If your stay is shorter than 6 months with a valid visa, an ECC is typically not required.
